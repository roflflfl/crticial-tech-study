Nazar Golianych is a moving-image artist and investigator. His aim is to explore new ways of sensing and making-sense of the world. Nazar maintains wip.network —a space for study groups that enables independent research with community funding. He is an Open Source Researcher volunteer for two investigative agencies - Molfar and TrapAgressor . He is a text-editor for Berlin-based art magazine local group. He works full time for Google, helping small Ukrainian business with their advertising strategy. Reach him via golianych.nazar@gmail.com.

At the break: Territorialization of Arctic

<investigation>

This investigation examines Russia's transformation of the Arctic through extractivist infrastructure, using open-source tools to analyze four case studies: the Prirazlomnaya oil platform, the North Sea Passage, a transarctic fiber-optic cable, and military expansion along the northern border. As climate change accelerates ice loss, Russia leverages new opportunities for control, reorienting the Arctic's spatial and economic networks toward East Asia as a key geopolitical strategy in the 21st century.

Economies of the aftermath

<investigation>

Examination of economic life in the wake of destruction, challenging the conventional notion of reconstruction. Through a lens of negative archaeology, investigation explores how violence doesn't just disrupt economies — it reshapes them entirely. The study of Mariupol's economic collapse under occupation reveals how infrastructure destruction and resource deprivation are tools of economic colonization. What emerges is a fragmented system — bartering, black markets, forced dependencies—where survival is conditioned by control. Developed for AWCJournal Issue 1: Entanglements of Infrastructures.

DISARM

<moving image>

"DISARM" follows an information warfare specialist assigned to produce materials for cognitive assaults. Created with custom-built montage software, the work explores how vicarious trauma is strategically inflicted through operational images. In the process of the creation, a database of 7,500,000 messages and 1,000,000 visual elements related to information operations was collected using custom Telegram scrapers. It is work-in-progress, expected to be finished by July 2025.

After Orgy Exhibition

<moving image>

When visual sensation surpasses the limits of sense-making, the post-image world emerges. The questions that follow are: how did this rupture occur, what does it mean to see images, and what can be done in the aftermath? "Every beautiful image I see is blurry because of the irreparable damage done to my retinas" is a moving image work developed for the After Orgy 003 exhibition at Pharmakon Gallery in Bucharest.

Polyflux Game

<installation>

Polyflux is a game developed for the exhibition "Mom, Post-Internet is Not a Phase" in Athens. In Polyflux, a phoenix emerges from the ashes of an overheated computer, overwhelmed by an excess of spawned butterflies. With each system reboot, the game offers a new perspective, transforming the crash into a generative cycle of digital rebirth.
